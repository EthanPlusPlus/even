# Problem

The moment Even exists for.

---

You're at a restaurant. A huge table of your friends, chilling, having a great time. But when it comes time to pay, the mood of the whole table drops — hungry stomachs are full, energy is depleted, topics of conversation are exhausted.

And now you still have to pass the bill around the table, do mental maths from grade 6, and face the sins of your night by paying your amount.

---

## Why does this suck?

### 1. You're tired

You've spent the night socialising and enjoying the moment.

**Deeper:** We care about our people. We want to share fun experiences with them — not end the night with admin.

### 2. Human error in the maths

People mess up. Mental maths is hard.

**Deeper:** Decimals and rounding bring unnecessary complexity. Who cares if someone pays R1.30 extra?

### 3. Unaccounted portions

There's often a gap, and a nice person covers it.

**Deeper:** A mix of negligence and kindness — but no real accountability.

### 4. The experience itself is boring and mundane

You're passing around a long piece of paper with tiny numbers on it and a pen, forcing people to read it and do math. This repeats every time you do something with your group.

**Deeper:** The system is old. Because the amounts and proportions people pay are **variable**, individual amounts get punched into the machine one by one.

### 5. The shock of how much you spent

You don't want to think about that straight after having a good time.

**Deeper:** It mixes the **experience** with the **consequence**.

> Open question: maybe that's a good thing — worth looking into.

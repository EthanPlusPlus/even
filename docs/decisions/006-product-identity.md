# 006 — Product Identity

Date: 2026-04-30

## Decision

Even is not just a bill-splitting tool.

**Even is the closing ritual of a shared experience involving money.**

## What Even is

- A tool for closing moments cleanly
- A lightweight social interaction
- A shared reflection point (brief, not persistent)

## What Even is NOT

- A debt tracker (no cross-receipt balances)
- A financial management tool
- A social media platform
- A real-time collaborative system (for now)
- A payment processor (for now)

## Emotional goal

The intended emotional outcome is **nostalgia and recognition** during the process. Relief and fairness are expected but not the focus.

Users should feel:

- "Oh yeah, we did order that"
- "Wait, you had that?? 😭"
- "That night was actually great"

The act of splitting should reinforce the experience — not interrupt it.

## The allocation screen is the product

Allocation is not just functional — it is the product's core interaction moment.

**Structural model:** tap item → select people → equal split within selection (unchanged, per decision 003).

**Emotional model:** allocation should feel like *remembering and confirming*, not *assigning and calculating*.

Design implications:

- Items should feel like distinct, scannable "moments" — not dense rows
- Person selection must be instant and lightweight — no friction, no modals
- Feedback should be subtle but satisfying — small confirmations, not loud animations
- Progress should feel natural, not task-driven

## Strategic positioning

Even's long-term defensibility is not in OCR, splitting logic, or payments.

It is in **owning the emotional and social dynamic of the bill moment**.

This is intentionally something large platforms (e.g. Apple) are less likely to prioritise or execute well.

## Why

The casual-yet-essential principle (`problem.md`) is only achievable if the product knows precisely what it is. A bill-splitting tool is a utility. A closing ritual is an experience. The difference shapes every design and scope decision downstream.

## Revisit when

- The product shape shifts to multiplayer-at-the-table (decision 001) — at that point the "closing ritual" must still be preserved even in a live-collaborative context.
- Payment integration becomes real — must not introduce fintech-first framing.
